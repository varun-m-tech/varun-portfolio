Who I am

The mind of an engineer.
The patience of a teacher.

I work late and I work precisely — the focus of a night owl, the steadiness of someone who means it. I started at the front of a classroom teaching SQL, and that taught me the thing most engineers learn too late: if you can't explain it simply, you don't really understand it.

Quality, to me, isn't a phase at the end of a release. It's a discipline that runs through everything — clear thinking, honest testing, and ownership that doesn't end when the bug is filed.

Precision over speed

Done right beats done fast. Flaky tests and shortcuts are debt I refuse to take on.

Teach, don't gatekeep

Knowledge shared makes a stronger team. I document and mentor by default.

Own the outcome

From requirement to production, I stay accountable for whether it actually works.
